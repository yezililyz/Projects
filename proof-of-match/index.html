🎯 01 — Overview

The mission:
trusted, deal-driven relationships

Proof of Match is a high-end dealflow enabler — an engine for high-intent, small-room events where capital, infrastructure, and policy decisions are made. The platform's goal is to become the driver of trusted, deal-driven relationships for private-market decision-makers.

Where existing solutions rely on keyword search and filter grids, Proof of Match layers GenAI enrichment, semantic embeddings, and persona-aware scoring to surface the five or ten meetings worth having — not a hundred maybes.

🤖

4 GenAI agent layers

Data enrichment → Embeddings → Individual scoring → Pair scoring & contextual intro generation.

👤

3 Persona archetypes

Investors, Founders, and Regulators — each with distinct mandates and weight vectors.

⚖️

Bidirectional scoring

A→B ≠ B→A. Each person's shortlist reflects their own perspective, not a generic match score.

📋

4-stage user journey

Registration → Onboarding → Pre-event matching & scheduling → Final schedule & on-site guidance.

🌐 02 — Context & Vision

Design by accretion —
the keyword matching trap

High-intent small-room event organisers in the Web3 and Venture Capital space bring capital, infrastructure, and policy decision-makers together with one shared objective: deal-ready conversations. But the current networking infrastructure relies on keyword matching and search filters — and produces two systematic failure modes.

⬆️

Surface-level matches

An investor is matched with a founder who shares the same thesis and intent — but is at a completely different raising stage. No deal is possible in the current cycle.

👁️

Missed non-obvious connections

Founders solving similar infrastructure problems rarely volunteer that context on a registration form. The best introductions — driven by inherent secondary signals — are invisible to keyword systems.

"The current platform optimises for volume of introductions. Our goal is to optimise for the three conversations that actually move a deal forward."

Product Vision — Proof of Match

The vision is to make the platform the driver of trusted, deal-driven relationships for private-market decision-makers. Proof of Match is a high-end dealflow enabler: an engine for high-intent, small-N room events where capital, infrastructure, and policy decisions are made.

👥 03 — Users & JTBD

Three personas, one room,
very little time

Every attendee at a high-intent VC event belongs to one of three decision-making archetypes. Their mandates overlap in important ways — and diverge in critical ones. The platform must serve all three simultaneously in a compressed time window.

💼

Investors & Allocators

Deploy capital
  • Identify thesis-fit, deal-ready founders and co-investors to add to the pipeline
  • Understand where capital, regulation, and infrastructure are moving to refine their own thesis
🚀

Founders & Operators

Raise & grow
  • Secure strategic capital and a handful of right partners — design, distribution, infra, regulatory
  • Build a targeted investor map they can nurture long after the event
🏛️

Regulators & Policymakers

Shape frameworks
  • Find serious builders and allocators to inform frameworks and potential sandboxes
  • Map the ecosystem's systemically important actors and risk areas efficiently

🔍 Research Approach

Research drew from three streams: interviews with VC event attendees across all three personas; LinkedIn fundraising guides and founder playbooks; and analysis of registration form completion patterns from the anonymised conference data. The goal was to identify which signals actually predict whether someone will take a conversation seriously — and which ones are currently invisible to keyword-based matching.

⚡ 04 — Pain Points

Three prioritised pain points
across all personas

Across all three archetypes and the context of a short, high-stakes time window, the same underlying frustrations surface consistently — regardless of role.

🎯 P1 · Highest priority

Right counterpart, actually ready

"I struggle to find the few people who are both a strong strategic fit and truly ready to do a real deal or pilot in the next cycle — instead of spending time on high-level chats that go nowhere."

The gap between thesis alignment and deal readiness is the most common source of wasted time at these events. The platform must surface readiness signals — not just fit signals.

⏱️ P2 · High priority

Limited time, multiple intents

"My time is extremely limited and I have several goals — deploy, raise, find partners, learn — so I need help prioritising a small number of meetings I can turn into long-term relationships."

Attendees carry simultaneous objectives. The platform must help them triage to the two or three meetings most worth having — not maximise random introductions.

🕵️ P3 · Medium priority

Non-obvious, cross-persona connections

"I often miss non-obvious but high-value connections across roles and verticals because current tools only match on surface keywords and titles."

Some of the highest-value introductions happen between attendees who don't look like obvious matches — a regulator and an infra founder solving the same underlying problem, for instance.

📡 05 — Connection Signals

What actually makes people connect
in VC & Web3

From interviews, LinkedIn deep-dives, and founder playbooks, these are the signals that most reliably determine whether someone will take a conversation seriously at a VC/Web3 event. Stage and fund-size alignment is necessary but not sufficient — it gets you into the "maybe" bucket, not the "must meet" bucket.

01

🏷️ Thesis fit first [1]

Clear match on sector, stage, geography, and check or token economics. This is the prerequisite — not the differentiator. It screens out mismatches but doesn't create meetings worth having on its own.

02

🤝 Warm intros & shared nodes [2]

Successful raises consistently trace back to warm introductions through portfolio founders or mutual connections. Cold approaches lag far behind. Shared relationship nodes are the hidden architecture of deal flow.

03

⚙️ Proof of work [2]

Visible shipped product, live code, traction metrics, or serious regulatory engagement. This separates serious actors from tourists — and makes every persona lean in, because it de-risks the conversation before it starts.

04

🎯 Targeted, informed outreach [3]

Showing you understand the other side's mandate — fund thesis, startup runway, regulatory remit — makes the conversation feel like collaboration rather than a sales pitch. Specificity signals respect.

🏗️ 06 — Data Strategy & Architecture

From sparse registration data
to deal-ready signals

The initial registration form collects only sparse data: name, company, title, ticket type, self-selected interests, and a free-text intent or mandate field. That gives us enough to infer persona archetype and broad intent — but not the richer signals that actually drive deal-flow conversations.

💡 The enrichment gap

"The real drivers of deal-ready conversations — credibility, financial and operational readiness, funding stage, check size, geographic focus, thesis, vertical, regulatory context, and latent secondary intents — require enrichment from public sources. GenAI agents fill that gap."

🤖 Four GenAI Agent Layers

1

📥 Data enrichment

Extracts entities from unstructured public data — LinkedIn, company websites, on-chain activity, regulatory filings — and outputs structured fields: stage, check size, vertical, geography, credibility signals, proof-of-work indicators.

2

🧬 Embedding & semantic similarity

Generates vector embeddings of enriched profiles and computes cosine similarity across thematic overlap, primary intent, and inferred secondary intent — catching non-obvious connections that keyword search would miss entirely.

3

📊 Individual scoring

Scores each attendee on credibility, financial readiness, and operational readiness dimensions.

⚠️ LLMs can hallucinate and carry biases. Strict scoring definitions, human-in-the-loop review, and guardrails are required before deployment.
4

✉️ Pair scoring & contextual intro generation

Scores each attendee pair on persona fit, theme overlap, intent overlap, non-obvious match score, and complementary fit. Translates structured outputs into warm-intro language surfaced at the UI layer — actionable context, not raw scores.

🗂️ ML Candidate Generation — Two Buckets

With enriched signals, the candidate generation layer classifies potential pairs into two priority buckets before scoring and ranking:

🎯 Complementary matching

High-precision matches on intent, persona, and thesis. These are the "must meet" introductions — strong signal on all the right dimensions.

💡 Non-obvious bucket

Problem-space matches across personas — two founders solving the same infrastructure challenge, or a regulator with an adjacent vertical builder. Lower precision, higher surprise value.

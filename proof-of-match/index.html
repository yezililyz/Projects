Proof of Match — AI-powered deal-ready matching for VC & Web3 events

Self-initiated AI product case study exploring how a multi-agent matching system can turn high-intent VC & Web3 events into engines for deal-ready conversations, not just random introductions.

Confidentiality

For confidentiality, this case leaves out business-sensitive details and competitor data. It is inspired by a real Web3 VC conference brief; all sensitive elements have been anonymized or omitted. The focus is on product thinking and AI architecture.

01 — Overview

What this product tries to solve

High-intent, small-room events in Web3 and venture capital bring together allocators, founders, infrastructure providers, and policymakers with the explicit goal of doing deals, pilots, and meaningful collaborations. Today, most networking tools still rely on simple keyword search and static filters.

The result: matches that look plausible in a spreadsheet but are neither deal-ready nor operationally aligned, while non-obvious, high-value connections are never surfaced. Proof of Match explores how an AI-first matching engine could re-rank the room around real deal-readiness and shared mandates, rather than just job titles.

08 — Prototype

Prototype

A lightweight prototype implements a simplified version of the enrichment, scoring, and ranking logic. It takes synthetic attendee profiles and intents, runs them through chained AI calls, and surfaces ranked match lists with contextual explanations.
